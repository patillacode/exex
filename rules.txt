🎯 Game Title: Expresión Exprés
🧠 Game Objective
Two teams compete to guess words or expressions. A team wins by moving its token along a board and reaching the finish line first.

🎲 Components
A word selector (a device or virtual tool that shows a new word/expression each time it's activated).

A timer that increases in speed and ends with a buzzer sound.

A circular board with spaces from START to FINISH.

16 double-sided word disks (each with one expression/word).

2 team tokens (one per team).

A set of game rules.

🔧 Setup
Players form two teams.

Each team selects a token and places it on the START space of the board.

Insert the word disks into the selector in the predefined order.

Turn on the timer. It starts counting with a progressively faster beeping sound.

One player from the starting team holds the selector and prepares to describe the word shown.

🎮 Game Flow
On Each Turn:
The active team sees a word on the selector.

One team member (the clue-giver) must describe the word to their teammate (the guesser).

The guesser must identify the word before the timer runs out.

Rules for Clue-Giving:
Do NOT use rhymes or sound-alikes (e.g., saying “singer” if the word is “sing” is not allowed).

Do NOT use word roots or part of the word (e.g., saying “dish” for “dishwasher” is not allowed).

Success:
If the team guesses the word correctly before the timer buzzes:

The team advances their token by 1 space.

The selector and turn pass to the opposing team.

Failure:
If the timer buzzes while the team is still trying to guess:

The opposing team earns 1 point (moves their token 1 space forward).

The Timer:
The beeping accelerates over time, creating pressure.

When the buzzer sounds (e.g., a long “beeeep”), the turn ends immediately.

🏁 Winning the Game
The first team to move their token all the way to the FINISH space wins.

💡 Implementation Tips (for a web version)
Word Pool: Use a JSON or database list of expressions.

Timer: Use increasing intervals with sound cues.

Game State: Store team turns, guessed words, and token positions.

Frontend: Use simple UI with a timer, word display, and board.

Rules Enforcement: Optional manual or AI-assisted flagging of invalid clues.
